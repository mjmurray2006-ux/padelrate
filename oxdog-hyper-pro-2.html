Verdict

The Oxdog Hyper Pro 2.0 punches well above its price tag. This Scandinavian brand brings serious padel expertise to a racket that delivers impressive performance at a mid-range price, making it one of the best value propositions in the current market.

Full Review

Oxdog might not have the same global marketing presence as Adidas or Babolat, but in Scandinavia and across the European padel community, they have built a strong reputation for producing technically sound rackets at honest prices. The Hyper Pro 2.0 is a compelling example of what the brand does well.

The teardrop shape sits at the heart of the racket's character. It is designed to offer offensive capability without the demands of a full diamond, which makes it well-suited to intermediate players pushing towards the advanced level or advanced players who prefer a more versatile frame over a pure attacking weapon. The medium-high balance point nudges the sweet spot slightly upward compared to a round racket, adding punch to overhead play without making the racket feel unwieldy.

Oxdog's HybridFoam core is one of the more interesting technical choices in this price bracket. It blends firmer and softer foam compounds in a layered construction designed to provide power on harder strikes while retaining feel and touch on softer, more controlled shots. In practice, this works well. The Hyper Pro 2.0 feels alive under the ball, responsive and communicative without being harsh.

Control is one of this racket's genuine strengths. The string pattern and core combination give you enough dwell time to redirect the ball accurately, and defensive shots from the back of the court feel stable and predictable. The touch at the net is above average for the price point, making it a strong choice for players who like to construct points through the net game.

At around 179, the Hyper Pro 2.0 represents outstanding value. Players at intermediate level looking for a meaningful upgrade from a beginner racket, or those who want a reliable second racket for training, will find it hard to beat at this price. Oxdog's build quality is solid, and the racket will comfortably handle the demands of regular club play over a full season.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Excellent value for money
  • HybridFoam core balances power and touch well
  • Versatile teardrop suits a wide range of styles
  • Above-average control and net game feel

Cons

  • Less brand recognition than market leaders
  • Not a top choice for pure power players
  • Medium-high balance is a compromise, not a speciality

Best For

Intermediate players looking for a high-performing all-round teardrop racket at a mid-range price, or advanced players who want a versatile second racket without the premium cost.
