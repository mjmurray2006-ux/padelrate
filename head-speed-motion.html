Verdict

The Head Speed Motion is a teardrop padel racket built for intermediate players who prioritise control and touch. With the highest touch score in our database at 90, it is the go-to choice for players focused on precision, net game and consistent placement.

Full Review

Head have long been respected in padel for building rackets that feel accessible without feeling cheap, and the Speed Motion is a perfect example of that philosophy. It prioritises exceptional touch, control and feel — the attributes that become increasingly valuable as your game develops and you start to demand more precision from your racket.

The teardrop shape is well-chosen for this racket's character. Sitting between the fully forgiving round and the demanding diamond, it positions the sweet spot slightly above centre, adding more punch and drive potential than a pure round while remaining considerably more forgiving than a diamond. For intermediate players who have outgrown an entry-level round, this is an ideal transition shape.

The EVA Soft Foam core plays a big role in the racket's character. Softer foam absorbs more energy, which gives you a cushioned, connected feeling when striking the ball. It also makes the racket very arm-friendly over long sessions — a genuine advantage for players who train regularly. The low balance point keeps the weight feeling centralised, making it quick to manoeuvre and comfortable to swing repeatedly.

On court, the Speed Motion excels in control and placement. Short game exchanges, lobs and defensive resets all feel natural. The touch at the net is excellent, and you can direct the ball with confidence even when you are still building consistency. Power is naturally limited compared to more aggressive teardrop and diamond frames, but that is the deliberate trade-off for a racket built entirely around touch and precision.

For the price, the Speed Motion represents outstanding value. Head's build quality ensures it will hold up through a full season of regular club play. If you are an intermediate player who prioritises consistent placement, net game and arm comfort over power, this is the racket we would recommend.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Teardrop shape balances forgiveness with added drive potential
  • Excellent touch and control
  • Soft EVA core is arm-friendly
  • Great value for money

Cons

  • Limited power for offensive play
  • Power ceiling becomes apparent at advanced level
  • Plain aesthetics compared to higher-end models

Best For

Intermediate players who prioritise control, touch and net game — and players stepping up from an entry-level frame who want exceptional feel without sacrificing too much forgiveness.
